The OR operator in SQL is used to combine multiple conditions, but unlike AND, it returns rows when at least one condition is TRUE.

✅ Basic Syntax

SELECT column_name
FROM table_name
WHERE condition1 OR condition2;

🔹 1. Simple OR Condition
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE City = 'Pune' OR City = 'Mumbai';

👉 Explanation:
Shows employees from:
Pune OR
Mumbai
If any one condition is true → row is included

🔹 2. OR with Multiple Conditions
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE City = 'Pune' OR Salary > 50000 OR Age < 22;

👉 Explanation:
Returns rows if:
City is Pune
OR salary > 50000
OR age < 22

🔹 3. OR with AND (Important)
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE City = 'Pune' AND Salary > 30000 OR Age < 25;

👉 Explanation:
SQL evaluates AND first:
(City = 'Pune' AND Salary > 30000) OR Age < 25
So:
Employees from Pune with salary > 30000
OR any employee with age < 25

🔹 4. OR with Brackets (Best Practice)
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE City = 'Pune' AND (Salary > 30000 OR Age < 25);

👉 Explanation:
First bracket is evaluated:
Salary > 30000 OR Age < 25
Then:
Must be from Pune

🔹 5. OR with BETWEEN
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE Age BETWEEN 20 AND 30 OR Salary > 40000;

👉 Explanation:
Age between 20–30
OR salary > 40000

🔹 6. OR with LIKE
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE Name LIKE 'A%' OR Name LIKE 'B%';

👉 Explanation:
Names starting with A
OR names starting with B

🔹 7. OR with NULL
SELECT * 
FROM Employees
WHERE Email IS NULL OR Phone IS NULL;

👉 Explanation:
Shows employees with:
Missing email
OR missing phone

🔹 8. OR with UPDATE
UPDATE Employees
SET Salary = 60000
WHERE Department = 'IT' OR Department = 'HR';

👉 Explanation:
Updates employees from:
IT
OR HR

🔹 9. OR with DELETE
DELETE FROM Employees
WHERE City = 'Mumbai' OR Age < 20;

👉 Explanation:
Deletes employees:
From Mumbai
OR age < 20

🔹 10. OR with JOIN
SELECT e.Name, d.DepartmentName
FROM Employees e
JOIN Departments d
ON e.DeptID = d.DeptID
WHERE d.Location = 'Pune' OR e.Salary > 50000;

👉 Explanation:
Show:
Employees in Pune department
OR employees with high salary

🔹 Truth Table of OR
TRUE OR TRUE = TRUE
TRUE OR FALSE = TRUE
FALSE OR TRUE = TRUE
FALSE OR FALSE = FALSE

✅ Real-Life Example
"I will go out if it is Sunday OR I have money"

Only one condition needs to be true.

✅ AND vs OR (Quick Difference)
AND	OR
All conditions must be true	Any one condition must be true
Strict filtering	Loose filtering


✅ Interview Answer
OR operator is used to combine multiple conditions in SQL and returns records when at least one condition is true.
