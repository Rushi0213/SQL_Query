The BETWEEN operator in SQL is used to select values within a range (inclusive) — meaning it includes both start and end values.

✅ Basic Syntax
SELECT column_nameFROM table_nameWHERE column_name BETWEEN value1 AND value2;

🔹 1. Simple BETWEEN Query
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Age BETWEEN 20 AND 30;

👉 Explanation:
Returns employees whose age is 20 to 30 (including 20 and 30)



🔹 2. BETWEEN with Numbers
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Salary BETWEEN 25000 AND 50000;

👉 Explanation:
Shows employees with salary between 25k and 50k



🔹 3. BETWEEN with Dates
SELECT * FROM OrdersWHERE OrderDate BETWEEN '2024-01-01' AND '2024-12-31';

👉 Explanation:
Returns orders placed in the year 2024



🔹 4. BETWEEN with Text
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Name BETWEEN 'A' AND 'M';

👉 Explanation:
Shows names starting from A to M (alphabetical range)



🔹 5. NOT BETWEEN
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Age NOT BETWEEN 20 AND 30;

👉 Explanation:
Shows employees:
Less than 20
OR greater than 30



🔹 6. BETWEEN with AND Condition
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Age BETWEEN 20 AND 30 AND City = 'Pune';

👉 Explanation:
Age between 20–30
AND city must be Pune



🔹 7. BETWEEN with OR
SELECT * FROM EmployeesWHERE Age BETWEEN 20 AND 25 OR Salary > 50000;

👉 Explanation:
Age between 20–25
OR salary greater than 50k



🔹 8. BETWEEN with UPDATE
UPDATE EmployeesSET Salary = 40000WHERE Age BETWEEN 25 AND 35;

👉 Explanation:
Updates salary for employees aged 25–35



🔹 9. BETWEEN with DELETE
DELETE FROM EmployeesWHERE Salary BETWEEN 10000 AND 20000;

👉 Explanation:
Deletes employees with low salary (10k–20k)



🔹 Important Rule
BETWEEN includes both values20 AND 30 → includes 20 and 30

✅ Real-Life Example

"Select students whose marks are between 40 and 80 (including both)"


✅ Interview Answer
BETWEEN operator is used to filter data within a range and includes both starting and ending values.
